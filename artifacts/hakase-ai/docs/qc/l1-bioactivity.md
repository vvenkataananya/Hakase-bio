## What this measures

The L1 dose-response engine (`src/lib/inVitroSim.ts`) emits a predicted
pIC50 from physicochemical descriptors plus an optional therapeutic-area
pharmacophore bonus.  This harness compares those predictions against
DrugCentral 2021_09_01 literature IC50 / Ki / Kd values for the **179
inhibition-compatible UniProts in our validated-target allow-list**.

The harness is **internal QC only** — its outputs are never shown to
users.  No L1 numbers in the product are derived from the harness or
from any individual DrugCentral row.

## Method

- **Data**: `drug.target.interaction.tsv` ⨝ `structures.smiles.tsv` on `STRUCT_ID`.
- **Filter**: `ACCESSION` ∈ allow-list (single-accession, human), `ACT_TYPE` ∈ {IC50, Ki, Kd, pIC50, pKi, pKd}, finite positive `ACT_VALUE`.
- **Unit handling**: IC50/Ki/Kd are nM in this dump (ACT_UNIT is blank — per DrugCentral schema); p-forms are already −log10(M).  Both converted to pIC50.
- **Two scoring arms**:
  1. `predBase` — `computeBaselinePIC50` only (no TA, no pharmacophore bonus). Measures the pure physchem prior.
  2. `predPartial` — `computeBaselinePIC50 + computePharmacophoreBonus(pharm, TA)` where TA is the default per target class (Kinase→Oncology, Enzyme→Oncology, Ion channel→CNS).
- **Clamp**: `predPartial` is clamped to [4.0, 9.5] (matches `runInVitroSimulation`).

> `predPartial` is **not** the full runtime prediction: the production
> `runInVitroSimulation` also applies a therapeutic-area `taMult`
> potency-multiplier term from `getTherapeuticProfile` that this
> harness does not replicate.  In practice that term shifts the
> predicted pIC50 by a few tenths of a log unit per TA, so absolute
> error here is a lower-bound estimate of the full runtime calibration
> gap.  Rank-order metrics (Pearson r, Spearman ρ, within-±k) are
> unaffected because `taMult` is per-TA, not per-compound.

Note: this is a calibration benchmark, **not** a predictive-modelling
metric.  The predictor was not fit on this data; both arms therefore
have explicit prior assumptions that bias the absolute scale (in
particular, `predBase` rarely exits the 5.0–6.5 window).  Use MAE,
Pearson r and the within-±1/±2 columns rather than R²: a model with
reasonable rank-order but a constant offset will show low R² but
useful Pearson r.
