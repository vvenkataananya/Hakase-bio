Yes — this is actually the best direction if you want to build a serious computational ADMET stack without depending on commercial black boxes.

Here are the strongest **open/pretrained models and ML architectures** by category.

# Foundation Chemistry Models (MOST IMPORTANT)

These are the backbone models you should fine-tune across ADMET tasks.

## Transformer Models + Molecular Language Models

### ChemBERTa

* SMILES transformer
* pretrained on PubChem
* HuggingFace compatible
* excellent for transfer learning

[ChemBERTa Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.09885?utm_source=chatgpt.com) ([arXiv][1])
[DeepChem ChemBERTa Tutorial](https://deepchem.io/tutorials/transfer-learning-with-chemberta-transformers/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) ([deepchem.io][2])

Best use:

* hepatotoxicity
* BBB
* CYP classification
* hERG
* bioavailability

---

### MolFormer

Large-scale molecular transformer.

Advantages:

* much better long-range chemical representation
* scaffold generalization
* strong transfer learning

Good for:

* multitask ADMET
* target affinity
* metabolism

---

### GROVER

Graph transformer pretrained on millions of molecules.

Excellent for:

* toxicity
* PK
* multi-endpoint prediction

---

# hERG Prediction Models

## CLOP-hERG

Contrastive learning pretrained hERG model.

Very strong modern architecture:

* pretrained molecular representations
* specifically optimized for hERG blockade

[CLOP-hERG Repository Index](https://drugbud-suite.github.io/CADD_Vault/ADMET/Pretrained%20Models/?utm_source=chatgpt.com) ([drugbud-suite.github.io][3])

---

## CardioTox

Deep ensemble cardiotoxicity predictor.

Uses:

* GNN ensembles
* toxicity-focused embeddings

Better than simple QSAR heuristics.

---

## Architecture Recommendation

Best open-source stack:

| Layer             | Model                   |
| ----------------- | ----------------------- |
| Molecular encoder | ChemBERTa / MolFormer   |
| Graph encoder     | MPNN / GAT / GIN        |
| Final predictor   | XGBoost or MLP ensemble |

This hybrid usually outperforms standalone models.

# CYP / Metabolism Models

This is where your current stack is weakest.

---

## [BioTransformer 3.0](https://biotransformer.ca/?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Best open metabolism framework right now. ([biotransformer.ca][5])

Capabilities:

* CYP450 metabolite prediction
* Phase I/II metabolism
* gut microbiome metabolism
* environmental metabolism

Uses:

* rule systems
* ML
* knowledge graph logic

This is MUCH stronger than SMARTS alerts.

---

## XenoSite

Very good for:

* CYP sites of metabolism
* reactive metabolite prediction

Predicts:

* likely oxidation sites
* bioactivation hotspots

---

## SyGMa

Rule + metabolism engine.

Still widely used.

---

## MetaPredictor

Transformer-style metabolite prediction.

Mentioned in CADD Vault index. ([drugbud-suite.github.io][3])

# General ADMET Multi-task Systems

## HelixADMET

One of the strongest open research systems.

Uses:

* self-supervised pretraining
* multitask transfer learning
* endpoint extensibility

Very aligned with modern pharma AI. ([arXiv][6])

[HelixADMET Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2205.08055?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

---

## ADMET-AI

Strong open multitask ADMET framework.

Predicts:

* absorption
* clearance
* toxicity
* distribution
* CYP

Referenced in ADMET model repositories. ([drugbud-suite.github.io][3])

---

## DeepChem

Probably the most important ecosystem.

[DeepChem Official Site](https://deepchem.io?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

Includes:

* GNNs
* transformers
* molecular featurizers
* docking interfaces
* ADMET datasets
* multitask pipelines

This should probably be your core framework.

# Best Model Architectures

## Best classical ML

Still surprisingly strong:

### XGBoost + Morgan fingerprints

Can outperform deep learning on small datasets.

[ADMET XGBoost Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.07532?utm_source=chatgpt.com) ([arXiv][7])

Very useful when:

* dataset < 50k compounds
* noisy labels
* sparse endpoints
