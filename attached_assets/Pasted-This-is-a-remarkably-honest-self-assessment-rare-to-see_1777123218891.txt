## The Real Strategic Question You Haven't Answered

You have two incompatible truths:

1. **The code is a sophisticated rule-based calculator** (accurate, defensible, but limited)
2. **The product is marketed as a BioDigital Twin** (implies prediction, simulation, digital replication)

Your priority list tries to bridge this gap with honesty and better plumbing. But **honest labeling doesn't make a calculator into a twin.** It makes it an *honest calculator*.

The actual choice is:

- **A) Narrow the claim:** "AI-powered triage engine for early drug discovery" — validated for hypothesis generation, not prediction. Sell to teams who need fast filtering, not accurate forecasting.
- **B) Expand the model:** Replace heuristic cores with trained ML models (or integrate external predictive APIs like ADMET Predictor, DeepPurpose, etc.) and validate rigorously. Sell to teams who need prediction, but now you're competing with established players.

You're currently in the middle — too honest to be a black box, too thin to be a real predictor. That's the hardest place to sell from.
