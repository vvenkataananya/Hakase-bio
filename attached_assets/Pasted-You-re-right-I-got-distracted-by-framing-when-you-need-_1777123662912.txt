## The Actual Strategic Problem

Your code produces weak signals. Your platform pushes them through four layers automatically. The cascade is the product's core architecture, but **the cascade amplifies error, not insight.**

Every L1 heuristic that feeds into L2 animal study configuration, L3 patient modeling, and L4 trial design carries uncertainty that compounds. You don't have a prediction problem. You have a **propagation of false confidence problem.**

The critic spotted this. Your own scorecard confirmed it. Now you need to decide what to do about it.

---

## Three Options, No Framing

**Option A: Keep the cascade, fix the inputs**

Replace heuristic cores with real data or validated models where possible. ChEMBL for binding. External predictors for ADMET. Keep the architecture, improve the signal quality.

- **Cost:** High. 4–6 weeks validation, API rewrites, potential model training.
- **Risk:** If validation fails, you've burned time and still have weak inputs.
- **Win condition:** R² > 0.6 on hERG/logP/Caco-2. Then you have a defensible predictor with a real cascade.

**Option B: Keep the inputs, kill the auto-cascade**

Heuristics stay. ChEMBL enrichment stays. But L1→L2→L3→L4 auto-configuration is gated or removed. Human review required at every layer transition. The tool becomes a hypothesis generator with workflow tracking, not an automated pipeline.

- **Cost:** Medium. UI changes, gating logic, review workflows.
- **Risk:** Slower user experience. Less "AI automation" appeal.
- **Win condition:** Users trust outputs because they know a human signed off. Liability drops. You sell governance, not prediction.

**Option C: Hybrid — tiered cascade**

Auto-cascade when data quality is high (ChEMBL experimental anchors, high confidence). Force review when data is heuristic-only. The architecture adapts to signal strength rather than treating all inputs equally.

- **Cost:** Medium-high. Data quality scoring, conditional routing, UI for two modes.
- **Risk:** Complexity. Two user paths to maintain.
- **Win condition:** Best of both. Speed when data supports it, safety when it doesn't.

---

## My Recommendation

**Option C, but with a specific implementation:**

1. **Data quality score per L1 output.** Not a prediction confidence — a *source quality* score.
   - Experimental (ChEMBL, measured): 1.0
   - Literature-derived (review articles, meta-analyses): 0.7
   - Heuristic (your rules): 0.3

2. **Cascade gate on minimum quality threshold.**
   - L1→L2 requires average quality ≥ 0.6 across all critical outputs (binding, ADMET, safety).
   - Below threshold: output is "triage-only," user must manually configure L2 or override with documented reason.

3. **No auto-cascade for NO-GO compounds.** Regardless of quality score. NO-GO means stop unless human explicitly overrides.

This is not about naming. This is about **structuring the product so it cannot make decisions it lacks data to support.**
